Curatorial Notes: The kula exchange system in the Trobriand Islands became famous with the 1922 publication of Bronislaw Malinkowski's Argonauts of the Western Pacific. The kula involves an inter-island network in which individuals customarily traded with partners in distant villages. Besides the regular articles of commerce, two types of tokens were exchanged to formally confirm the relationship between the partners. Necklaces of shell disks called soulava circulated clockwise, and armbands of cone shell called mwali circulated counter-clockwise from island to island (also see catalog number 80.1/3409). Except for three European glass beads, this soulava necklace is comprised almost entirely of shell disc beads made in the Trobriand Islands.

Curatorial Notes: The kula exchange system in the Trobriand Islands became famous with the 1922 publication of Bronislaw Malinkowski's Argonauts of the Western Pacific. The kula involves an inter-island network in which individuals customarily traded with partners in distant villages. Besides the regular articles of commerce, two types of tokens were exchanged to formally confirm the relationship between the partners. Necklaces of shell disks called soulava circulated clockwise, and armbands of cone shell called mwali circulated counter-clockwise from island to island (also see catalog number 80.1/3369).
