Curatorial Notes: [Label]
'MAORI STOREHOUSES (pataka) were carved in New Zealand for Maori chiefs to protect their valuables, including food, cloaks and weapons. Mounted on high poles near a chiefs house, a pataka declared prestige. This pataka was carved in the Rotorua region for a collector, circa 1908.
The storehouses carvings refer to prosperity and respect for ancestors. Look for two embracing couples (symbols of fertility) and the ancestor figure at the peak. There are whales on the sloping gables - their heads are the spirals at the lower corners. A beached whale signaled abundance, giving food and precious whalebone for carving.
Pataka are no longer made in New Zealand (Aotearoa) but carving and traditions of offering respect carry on today.'
Label for Margaret Mead Hall of Pacific Peoples, March 7 2013. Jenny Newell and Jacklyn Lacey.
