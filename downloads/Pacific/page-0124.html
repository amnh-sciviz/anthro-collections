Curatorial Notes: The Manus people of the Admiralty Islands made this beaded belt spelling "MAKRIT," their pronunciation of Margaret Mead's first name, and gave it to her when she visited them in 1953. The Manus also called Mead "Piyap," or "woman of the west." Mead originally worked amongst the Manus in the late 1920s, and visited them in 1953, 1964, 1965, and 1975. In 1956 she published New Lives for Old: Cultural Transformation - Manus, 1928-1953.
