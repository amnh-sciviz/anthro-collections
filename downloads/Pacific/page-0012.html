Curatorial Notes: Tatanua masks were worn by men during malangan ceremonies honoring the dead on the island of New Ireland. Carved from lime wood and elaborately painted, the masks were decorated with a variety of materials from the natural world, including snail shell opercula for the eyes. The placement of catalog numbers in prominent places, such as the foreheads of masks, was common in the 19th Century, when this mask was collected.
