Exhibition History
• BODY ART: MARKS OF IDENTITY. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. November 1999 - May 2000
Exhibition Label: Men’s Wig. Human hair, flowers, cassowary and other feathers, hide, fur, wood, leaves, vegetable fiber. 20th century. The Huli are sometimes referred to as “wig men” because of their practice of wearing wigs, which are woven out of men’s hair and beards. The down-turned wig with dried yellow flowers is an initiate’s headdress, worn to signal the passage from boyhood to adulthood.

Curatorial Notes: By lengthening a hunter's arm, spearthrowers improved both the distance and force of a spear. The designs carved into this spearthrower, made by the Ngatatjara Aborigines of Western Australia, are similar to those used in body painting.
Exhibition History
• HALL OF HUMAN ORIGINS PREP. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. August 2006
• BODY ART: MARKS OF IDENTITY. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. November 1999 - May 2000
Exhibition Label: Spearthrower. Wood, stone, resin, sinew, pigment. 1960s–1970s. A spearthrower effectively lengthened a hunter's arm when throwing a spear. The decorative patterns are similar to those used in body painting and serve a maplike function.
