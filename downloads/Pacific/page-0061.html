Material: WOOD, PIGMENT

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This is one of over seven hundred Arapesh artifacts collected by Margaret Mead during her 1931-1932 fieldwork in the Sepik River region of Papua New Guinea. This carving, representing an ancestor, would have been one of many used to decorate the interior of a ceremonial house. One of the publications resulting from Margaret Mead's 1931-1932 fieldwork is The Mountain Arapesh: an Importing Culture (1938).
Exhibition History
• BODY ART: MARKS OF IDENTITY. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. November 1999 - May 2000
Exhibition Label: Abelam figure. Wood, vegetable fiber, paint. 20th century. Decorated figures found inside ceremonial houses represent the spirits of clan ancestors. The face painting on these images is similar to that worn by Abelam men and women in ritual contexts.
