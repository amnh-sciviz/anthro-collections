Curatorial Notes: Thomas Gilliard, curator of ornithology at the American Museum of Natural History, was a leading expert on the bowerbirds and birds of paradise in Papua New Guinea. He and his wife Margaret were equally interested in the people of Papua New Guinea, and in the early 1950s they collected 268 objects, including this mask, while working in Kanganaman Village on the Sepik River. Jointly sponsored by the AMNH, the Explorer's club, and the National Geographic Society, Gilliard published three long and profusely illustrated articles about his work in National Geographic Magazine between 1953 and 1961.
