Exhibition Label: Some Han Dynasties (206 BC-AD 220) cooks used large, earthenware stoves fueled by wood, which created very high heat. Pots fit tightly into openings atop the stove; this close fit helped harness the fuel of the fire efficiently and allowed for high-temperature cooking. In rural China today, this type of stove is still used. This model stove was one of many ming qi objects - grave goods that accompanied the dead in the afterlife.
