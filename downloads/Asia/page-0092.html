Researchers' Remarks
Brocade and silk damask are two complicated weaves of silk with specific structures of warp (vertical) and weft (horizontal) threads woven on treadle looms. In this example, they are used together to make curtains for the doghouse collected from the Chinese imperial palace, Qing Dynasty (1644-1912). Nonetheless, imperial silks at that time were by no means widely consumed by majority Chinese people. They were mainly used for imperial attire, palace and temple furnishings, presentations and official dresses in the court. Therefore, this luxurious doghouse fitting shows not only the love for dogs but also a slice of life in the imperial court. The Qing textile department responsible for imperial silk production consisted of facilities both in the capital city of Beijing and in various regio. YUANXIE SHI. May 10, 2015.
