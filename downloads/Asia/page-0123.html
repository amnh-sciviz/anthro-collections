Researchers' Remarks
Camels are one of the most resilient mammals able to withstand extreme temperatures and their hairs are a sturdy source of fiber materials. This fiber was taken from a two-humped Bactrian camel. Camels grow two different types of hair, outer and inner. The outer hair of a camel is very tough and unable to be easily manipulated into yarn. The inner hair is very soft and flexible just like many other common animal fibers used to create yarns and textiles. The outer hair of a camel is rough to protect the animal from the extreme environmental conditions and elements while the inner wool serves as a source of additional protection and insulation for the animal. Camels usually produce five pounds of yarn a year while sheep produce around 7 or 8 pounds per year of usable fibers. The yarn on the spindle was made by first washing the wool and eliminating debris, carding the fibers, and then spinning the hairs to form one continuous string. Spinning the animal’s hair into this form strengthens it and turns it into a rope capable of being used for crafting, clothes and blankets. Because of the yarn’s thickness on this spindle, it was perhaps intended for a woven blanket. MELISSA CHASE. June 13, 2016.
