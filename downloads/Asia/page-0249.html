Curatorial Notes: Calendar of horoscope animals, a birthday gift to the Emperor Chien Lung (1711-1799 C.E.). Through the use of the sundial and of a variety of water clocks the Chinese, as early as 104 B.C.E., were able to establish a reliable calendar based upon the phases of the moon, the solstices and equinoxes and the movement and relative positions of the constellations. By 700 C.E. they had determined the length of the solar year at 365.2444 days. In addition, the Chinese designated animal symbols to each month, day, and hour of the year and related them to the trigrams of the I-Ching (Book of Changes), which refer to the measurement of chance. Each animal is regarded as exercising influence on one's birth and subsequent existence.
