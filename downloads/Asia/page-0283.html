Donor: CONKLIN, HAROLD C.
Keywords: FIGURE
Share Object

Curatorial Notes: Bulul figures represent mythological deities who assure an abundance of the rice on which the Ifugao subsist, and who protect the tribe’s complex system of rice terraces and granaries. Families prize their bulul figures, and selling or giving them away could cause a catastrophic crop failure. Accordingly, this male bulul figure, along with a female bulul figure (see #70.5/288), were specially made by the Ifugao in the early 1960s for Harold Conklin, a common solution when anthropologists wish to represent important features of a people's material culture which would otherwise be unavailable.
