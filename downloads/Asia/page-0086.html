Curatorial Notes: This Yukaghir shaman's hat is made from the short white-and-brown fur taken from the legs of reindeer and has the long fur tassels that were also characteristic of Even shaman's hats. The color division, white on the wearer's right side, dark on his left, signified the shaman's dual role, participating in the world of men (light) and the world of shades and spirits (darkness). Beads, dyed moosehair embroidery, and brass bells and rings complete the ornamentation. Yakut and Evenk shamans festooned their coats and hats with metal chains, spirit figures, and ornaments. These jingling attachments aided in attracting friendly spirits.
