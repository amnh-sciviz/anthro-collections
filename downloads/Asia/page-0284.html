Curatorial Notes: Dr. and Mrs. Charles Sibley collected this man's backpack between 1908 and 1915, when they were part of an American Congregationalist mission fighting malaria in Mindanao. Made of woven fiber from the abaca plant, the bag is decorated with glass beads and 179 brass bells made by Bagobo metalworkers. This bag produces a very pleasant sound when handled in Museum storage; when worn by a striding Bagobo man, the sound would have been even more pleasing.
