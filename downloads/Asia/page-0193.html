Curatorial Notes: In the Bunan village of Take-tonpo in central Taiwan, the parents of baby placed these teeth from a Formosan Clouded Leopard (Neofelis nebulas brachyurana) around their child’s neck to celebrate the child’s first full month of life. Amulets made from the remains of wild animals taken in the hunt were hung on a vulnerable young baby as necklaces or bracelets so that the animal’s fierceness would protect the child from harm. Although wild animal parts are no longer used, Bunan still protect a month-old baby with amulets.
