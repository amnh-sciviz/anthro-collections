Curatorial Notes: Prayer sticks (ikupasuy) are used by Ainu men for rituals to send messages to the gods and libation offerings. Carvings are usually left up to the owners discretion, however the crosswise cuts at the ends are for clan or family markings, so the gods can recognize the sender of the message. Designs on the sticks include geometric, landscape, floral, fish scale designs, whorl patterns and animal motifs are common; depictions of humans are never found.
