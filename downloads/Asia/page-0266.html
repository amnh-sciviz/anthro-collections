Exhibition Label: Pulse Examination. Traditional Tibetan diagnostic techniques include many forms of pulse reading. Pulses are often taken at the wrist, as in modern Western medicine, but the Tibetan practitioner uses three fingers instead of two. In Tibetan medicine, pulses are also taken on the patient’s fingers, on the feet and sometimes on other parts of the body. Taking the pulse requires very precise finger placement and long training to properly interpret each kind of pulse. Both doctor and patient are told to avoid inappropriate diet and behavior before pulse examination. Pulses taken at different points on the body are used to judge the health and humoral state of different organs. Here, the pulses of the heart and small intestine are examined at the thumb.
