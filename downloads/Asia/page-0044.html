Researchers' Remarks
This Kere'tkun sinew net was used in Kere'tkun ceremonial, an annual thanksgiving ceremonial at the end of fall honoring the Master of the Sea for the renewal of animals and hopes for future hunting success. It was used on land, spread out horizontally, and suspended from the smoke hole in the center of the dwelling with its edges fastened to the walls. A special pole was passed through the net and top of the smoke hole. With other magical objects that were believed to hold the spirit of umiak tied to it, this pole was believed to be the source of power of the ceremony. All around the net are suspended wooden models of gulls and paddles, painted ornamentally with seal blood. ZHIXUAN HUANG. April 25, 2019.
