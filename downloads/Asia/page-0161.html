SCABBARD
ASIAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 70.1/ 6143
Culture: BAGOBO
Locale: MINDANAO
Country: PHILIPPINES
Date: Early 20th Century A.D.
Acquisition Year: 1910 [PURCHASE]
Donor: BENEDICT, LAURA WATSON
Keywords: SCABBARD
Category: CONTAINERS
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: One of the many treasures of Bagobo material culture is this elaborate scabbard, in and of itself an attractive item, but additionally meaningful because of the story it tells about the effect of culture contact on material culture. The scabbard is constructed of locally-available wood, braided plant fiber and horse hair, but it is also decorated with imported European glass beads and the flattened metal remains of a Hohner harmonica. The scabbard was made sometime between 1900, the year stamped beneath "Paris" on the harmonica's cover plate, and 1908, the last year Laura Watson Benedict collected amongst the Bagobo. It did not matter the scabbard was missing its knife when Benedict collected it, for she knew good material culture when she saw it.
