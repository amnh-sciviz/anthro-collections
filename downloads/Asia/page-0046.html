Country: RUSSIA
Material: WOOD, SINEW, BONE
Dimensions: HARPOON L:55 W:.6 FLOATS L:17.5 W:6.5 (in CM)
Acquisition Year: 1902 [EXPEDITION]
Donor: BOGORAS, WALDEMAR
Keywords: MODEL HARPOON
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: Hunting whales is a dangerous activity requiring both courage and expertise where a wrong move could mean icy death for the entire hunting party. To ensure more successful hunts, Anqallyt people developed tools specifically adapted to the unique conditions of Arctic waters. Hunting parties first strike a whale with the harpoon, piercing through its blubber - a thick layer of insulating fat - to the skin beneath. The harpoons toggle-head point ensures that it remains embedded in the whale while allowing hunters to move the shaft away from floating ice and other obstacles. Floats made from inflated seal skins create drag in the water behind the whale, preventing it from escaping by sounding - diving from the surface into deeper waters.
