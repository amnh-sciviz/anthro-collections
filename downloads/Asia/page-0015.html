Researchers' Remarks
“These gloves showcase the craftsmanship of the Nanai (Golde), an ethnic group located along the banks of the Amur River between Russia and China. Traditionally a semi-nomadic people, the Nanai relied heavily on fishing and the fur trade for centuries, using fish as both a source of food and a source of material for making clothing and other goods. However, environmental changes and a depleted fish population forced the Nanai to abandon fish skin for a new material: wool. Made of sheep or goat wool, cotton, fur, metal, and thread, these gloves demonstrate the effects that agriculture and trade with other groups had on the Nanai’s traditions and material cultures in the 20th century. As the Nanai did not produce their own wool, its presence in these gloves shows the reliance of nomadic groups on trade with other peoples. Beautifully blending traditional Amur motifs with Chinese artistry, these gloves are evidence of not only material trade, but cultural exchange as well. Combining functionality and aesthetics, these gloves are indicative of the forces that affect changes in material culture: foreign influence, modernization, and trade.”. MICHELE CARLUCCI. June 13, 2016.

Curatorial Notes: Decorative salmon skin coats were worn as summer garments by peoples of the Lower Amur River. Those worn by women were especially elaborate. Decoration on the fronts of these coats was limited to border designs; those on their backs were more complex, composed of symmetrical band and figure dcor, often representing abstract cocks and fish. Designs were made with patterns and stencils that were passed down from mother to daughter. The designs and wraparound cut of Lower Amur area garments reflect Chinese rather than Siberian stylistic influence.
