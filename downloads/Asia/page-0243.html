Curatorial Notes: In the highlands of Vietnam, Laos, and Southwest China, the Water Buffalo was an important part of Hmong warfare technology. This powder horn was carved from the crescent-shaped horn of a buffalo and would have been used to carry gunpowder. For several centuries, Hmong people crafted their own handmade gunpowder weapons, including flintlock rifles, cannons, and other accessories, continuing into the 1960s, when the United States provided modern weapons during the Vietnam/American War. The hides, horns, and bones of the water buffalo were not only used for warfare, but were also used for divination in magical and ritual practices.
