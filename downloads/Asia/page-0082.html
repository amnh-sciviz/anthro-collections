Exhibition History
• BODY ART: MARKS OF IDENTITY. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. November 1999 - May 2000
Exhibition Label: Shoes. Silk, cotton, linen, leather, metal, metallic threads, lacquer, glass, paper, wood, plastic. c. 1900. Bound feet were shaped to look like unopened lotus flower buds. To emphasize the delicacy and perceived beauty of their small feet, Chinese girls and women made "lotus shoes" of exquisite workmanship. Men were never supposed to see unbound feet, but were instead meant to gaze at the dazzling tiny shoes. Women even slept in soft-soled slippers to hide their feet from view at night.
