Researchers' Remarks
In many cultures hats have historically been used as rank identifiers. This Tibetan hat from the first half of the twentieth century certainly was used to show profession. This specific hat belonged to a middle ranked official in Tibet. This ranking means the person wearing it was well respected in his community. Each region of the area has a different iconic hat attached to it some ranging from lager hats to more decorative in nature with embellishments and colors and patterns sewn on, and others made from un-felted animal fur. Tibet is a land that focuses on raising livestock and agriculture, so animal’s fibers are naturally abundant to most people who live there especially when this beret hat was made. The hat retains only a small amount of the “Yellowish-green” as described in the collector’s notes which means that this hat was dyed but perhaps dyed without a mordant to make the color last. The state of this hat’s structure is an indicator that the crafter of this hat was very experienced with wool felting practices and procedures. MELISSA CHASE. June 13, 2016.
