Keywords: COAT (WOMAN)

Curatorial Notes: An elaborately decorated coat (tulli) made and worn by Nivitsaanaq, an Aivilik Eskimo (Inuit). Her nickname "Shoofly" is in white beads on the front of the coat below one of the shoes. She obtained the beads from whaling Captain George Comer who acquired this parka from her in the early 1900s and brought it to the Museum in 1906. It is part of Captain Comer's collection of over 4200 Eskimo objects. The parka has been exhibited and published many times.
