Researchers' Remarks
THE CATALOG MENTIONS THIS ITEM WITH A HANDFUL OF OTHER "APACHE" OBJECTS ACQUIRED AT SAN ILDEFONSO PUEBLO IN THE 1890S. THIS WOULD SEEM TO INDICATE THE LIKELIHOOD THAT THIS WEAPON IS SPECIFICALLY JICARILLA APACHE, BECAUSE THE JICARILLA WERE THE ONLY APACHE GROUP WHO WERE IN REGULAR CONTACT WITH NORTHERN PUEBLO GROUPS SUCH AS SAN ILDEFONSO. JOSEPH AP WILSON, PHD CAND. ANTHROPOLOGY, U. FL. February 7, 2010.
