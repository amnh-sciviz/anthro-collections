Curatorial Notes: A model totem pole carved by the Haida artist John Robson in 1901 was commissioned by John Swanton at the instigation of Franz Boas. It depicts a mythical sea wolf, a raven and a grizzly bear holding a person in its mouth. This pole was exhibited in Raven Travelling (Vancouver Art Gallery 2006) and Objects of Exchange (Bard Graduate Center 2011) and published in their catalogs as well as in Swanton 1905.

Researchers' Remarks
In the manuscript catalog, this carved chest is listed as a "grave box." According to Newcombe, it was initially meant for the burial of the chief of Skedans. However, the chest was "considered by his survivors as more suitable as a receptacle for blankets," meaning it was not used for remains. (Newcombe, letter to Boas, May 9, 1901 [AMNH Anthropology Archives acc. 1901-36.]) For further information, see Publication History, Objects of Exchange. LAURA ALLEN, CURATORIAL ASSOCIATE.
