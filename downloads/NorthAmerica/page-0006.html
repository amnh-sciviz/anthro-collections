Exhibition History
• BODY ART: MARKS OF IDENTITY. AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. November 1999 - May 2000
Exhibition Label: Labrets. Wood, abalone shell, copper alloy, stone, paint. Haida, Tligit. 19th century. Among the Northwest Coastal peoples, only women wore labrets, or lip ornaments, first inserted when a girl reached puberty. Increasingly large ornaments indicated her rise in status as a married woman. Even after large labrets disappeared, piercing remained part of a girls coming of age ceremony.
