Exhibition Label: HEMUSHIKWA DANCE TABLITA. Unknown artist (Zuni). Wood, paint, hide, sinew. c. 1915. Not all costumed dances are sacred magical rituals. Many Pueblo ceremonies are “Social Dances,” in which the costumes adorn ordinary humans. Whether the ritual is social or sacred, the costumes are decorated with symbols that attract rain. The steplike shapes on this tablita headdress, for example, represent clouds. Designs from tablitas are sometimes transferred to dolls and jewelry.
