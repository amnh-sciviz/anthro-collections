CRADLEBOARD, CARVED

Catalog No: 50.1/ 1542
Culture: IROQUOIS, MOHAWK?
Locale: NY, FRANKLIN COUNTY, SAINT REGIS RESERVATION

Dimensions: L:69 W:33 H:30 (in CM)

Curatorial Notes: Iroquois cradleboard when in use has a cover, usually of red broadcloth, decorated with beads and silver ornaments with rattles hanging on the bow. The painted floral design became popular after 1840 particularly among the Mohawk of St. Regis reservation where this baby carrier was collected. Part of the Erastus Tefft collection of over 1100 objects acquired by AMNH in 1910, it was exhibited in The Hollywood Indian (New Jersey State Museum, Trenton 1980-1981), People of the Long House (Roberson Center for the Arts and Sciences, Binghamton, NY 1981-1984) and A Forest in the City (The New York Botanical Garden, Bronx 2011-2012).
