Exhibition History
THE HORSE AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. May 2008 - January 2009
Exhibition Label: Saddle blanket. United States. c. 1900. In the 1800s, the Crow people of the northern Plains kept some of the area’s largest horse herds. The Crow traveled long distances to trade with allies, exchanging horses for other valuable goods. When a band moved camp, a wealthy Crow woman would adorn her horse head to tail with beautifully beaded buffalo or buckskin trappings. Women made their own saddles and bridles, though Spanish bits with dangling trim were traded from tribe to tribe.
