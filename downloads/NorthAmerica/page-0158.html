Exhibition Label: WOMANS BELT. Unknown artist (Kiowa-Apache). Hide, silver. 1800s. Navajo artists borrowed the idea for the concho belt from Native people of the southern Plains, such as the Kiowas and Comanches. Early Navajo conchos were threaded onto a narrow leather band through holes cut in the center. After Southwest artists mastered soldering, they attached metal shanks to the backs of the disks, replacing the central opening with a decorative motif.
