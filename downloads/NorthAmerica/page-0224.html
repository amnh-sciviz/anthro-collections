ANTICOCK COAT
NORTH AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 60 / 4440 A
Culture: ESKIMO, IGLULIK
Locale: IGLULIK, WEST COAST OF HUDSON BAY
Country: CANADA
Material: HIDE, FUR (REINDEER), CLOTH, SINEW
Dimensions: L:101 W:98 H:6 (in CM)
Acquisition Year: 1902 [EXPEDITION]
Donor: COMER, GEORGE, CAPT.
Keywords: COAT (SHAMAN)
Category: COSTUME
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: The design of this shaman Qingailisaq's coat was based on a vision in which he saw a mountain spirit wear a coat like this one. It is perhaps the best-known object in the Eskimo collection. Six copies of it have been made, some for the Aivilik Eskimo (Inuit) themselves, some for other museums. It has been exhibited and published and will be the centerpiece of a video documentary by Prof. Bernard Saladin d'Anglure. It is part of the large collection (over 4200 objects) that whaling Captain George Comer made in the 1890s and in the first years of 1900s.
