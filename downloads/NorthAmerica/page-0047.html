Acquisition Year: 1901
Donor: HUNT, GEORGE
Keywords: DISH, FEAST DISH
Categories: CONTAINERS, EQUIPMENT
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This feast dish, carved in the form of a killer whale with a detachable dorsal fin and a human, represents an underwater adventure of Siwidi, a Kwakiutl (Kwakwaka'wakw) culture hero. The dish is pair to 60/8528 AB. Collected by George Hunt, Franz Boas' field assistant, in 1901, it was exhibited in Objects of Bright Pride (Center of Inter-American Relations, et al. 1978-1990) and Objects of Exchange (Bard Graduate Center 2011) and published in their catalogs as well as in Aldona Jonaitis 1991.
Exhibition History
WHALES: GIANTS OF THE DEEP AMNH, NEW YORK, NEW YORK. March 2013 - January 2014
Exhibition Label: This carved dish represents Siwidi, a hero of the Kwakwaka'wakw people of British Columbia. On one of his adventures, Siwidi travels undersea in a canoe transformed into a killer whale. This dish once held fish oil, and a small tube inside it could spout during ceremonies, just as a whale sprays through its specialized "nostrils" - the blowhole on top of its head.
