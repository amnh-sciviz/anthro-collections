LADLE
AFRICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 90.1/ 9446
Culture: MANO?
Country: LIBERIA?, COTE D'IVOIRE?
Acquisition Year: 1951 [PURCHASE]
Donor: LEMAIRE, M.L.J.
Keywords: LADLE
Category: EQUIPMENT
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This sizable wooden object with its oval-shaped scoop is known as a carved ceremonial ladle or rice spoon. When a West African family invites a masquerade to appear, its leaders must ensure that women in the family are able to supply enough food for the accompanying feast, providing and cooking the rice in their granaries as well as gathering preparations for the sauce. Indeed, a woman who aspires to become the principal hostess for the feast will see a spirit belonging to a spoon within her dreams. Thus the ceremonial ladle is considered a sacred object as an embodiment of a mystical entity.

Once selected the hostess is acknowledged as the most hospitable woman of her lineage and parades throughout the community with family and friends holding the serving spoon as an emblem of her status which features a sculpted handle that reflects a womans form such as seen on this object, an image of her face and head. Created as a visual analogy, this sacred ladle honors the hostess and in fact all women as a source of food and lifethe spoons concave symbolizing a female womband the spoon itself becoming an aesthetically-pleasing extension of its well-respected guardian.

Researcher: Siu Ying Ng, 2013

Source
Visona, Monica, Robin Poynor & Herbert Cole. 2008. A History of Art in Africa, Second Edition. New Jersey: Pearson Education/Prentice Hall.
