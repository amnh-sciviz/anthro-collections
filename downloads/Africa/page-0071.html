[describes other purchase - see Field No. 70] ....Bone hair pins were then produced and, with a little difficulty bought in[?] at maya , though one hung[?]. We then turned attention to an anklet of copper and iron [Field No. 76], which held no particular attraction. He had himself bought it in a distant town, Mikunga, paying 3.50 for it. After some discussion, he let us have it and the pin for 4. He had one undecorated pin left. We did not much care for it-- both because of non-decoration, and size and quality. On our pointing out the lack of decoration, he said that was easily arranged if we would lend him a knife. A knife supplied, he went hunting in the garden and produced a bit of charcoal. He cut the pattern and rubbed in charcoal. He is on the line[?] of cloths and dollars for us." (96); it is unclear which "bone pin" is which in Field Nos. 71-75
