Curatorial Notes: "The strip-woven cloth called kente, made by the Asante peoples of Ghana and the Ewe peoples of Ghana and Togo, is best known of all African textiles. Its renown has spread internationally, so that is now one of the most admired of all fabrics in many parts of the world. Kente has its origins in the former Gold Coast of West Africa as festive dress for special occasions - traditionally worn by men as a kind of toga and by women as an upper and lower wrapper (19)." - Ross, Doran H. Wrapped in Pride: Ghanaian Kente and African American Identity. University of California Los Angeles, Fowler Museum of Cultural History, 1998.
