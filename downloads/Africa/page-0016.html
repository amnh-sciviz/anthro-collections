HOUSE GOD
AFRICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 26 / 368
Culture: AUCANER
Locale: SARA CREEK DISTRICT, VILLAGE OF PITCHONG
Country: DUTCH GUIANA
Material: WASP NEST
Dimensions: L:23 W:12 H:13 (in CM)
Subject: HOUSE GOD
Acquisition Year: 1928 [EXPEDITION]
Donor: GRANGER, MYRON AND KAHN, MORTON
Keywords: FIGURE
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: The Saramacca are one of six distinct groups of the Suriname Maroons: runaway slaves from the Dutch plantations who established independent communities inland from the coastal Dutch settlements. Their religion is centered around a supreme being (Gran Gadu) and the belief that jungles, rivers, snakes and most entities embody a spirit thus each village, house and person hosts a deity to which offerings are made. In Saramaccan communities, every family worships a House God. When compared to the intricate designs of everyday items, house deities are conceived within more basic forms: the object exhibited here, for example, was created simply from a wasp's nest.

Researcher: Emily Fite, 2012

Source
Kahn, M. C. 1929. "Notes on the Saramaccaner Bush Negroes of Dutch Guiana." American Anthropologist, 31:468-490.
