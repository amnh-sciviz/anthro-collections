Curatorial Notes: This is a brass figure from Benin, of a Fon woman with a mortar and pestle. The 3.5 million Fon people form a major cultural group in Nigeria and Benin. The Fon people were part of the Kingdom of Dahomey, created in the seventeenth century. The kingdom was known for its corps of female soldiers. Dahomey kings ruled over them until 1894 when the French seized control.
