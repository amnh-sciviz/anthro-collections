Curatorial Notes: This West African Yoruba beaded cloth looks like a garment worn by a pair of ibeji, or twins of noble or chiefly descent. The Yoruba are an ethnic group in West Africa consisting of 30 to 50 million people. In Yoruba culture, twins are called ibeji, and the deity that protects twins is called Orisha Ibeji. The incidence rate of dizygotic twins in Nigeria, the homeland of the Yoruba people, is more than 10 times greater than the global average - 45 per 1000 live births. - Knox, G. and Morley, D. (1960), Twinning in Yoruba Women. BJOG: An International Journal of Obstetrics and Gynaecology, 67: 981-984.

Curatorial Notes: The Senufo people, known alternatively as the Senefo, Sene, Senoufo, Synambll, Siena and Bamana people are an ethnic group located in Cote d’Ivoire, Mali and Burkina-Faso.
