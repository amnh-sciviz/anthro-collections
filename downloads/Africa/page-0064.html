The old chief was a little perplexed but treated us well. At almost the first house we saw a child about whose neck was hung a figure of wood, plainly and suggestively phallic.... In the last of the houses we found another child, plainly suffering, who had one of the phallic figures tied around his neck and with it a hollow nut shell or seed case the size of a medium potato. We had no[?] located what interested us in the village and summoned Mr. Bertrand for help. The two children were ill and wore these to cure their sickness. They were not made here but brought with them. The capita probably keeps them, but declared he could not sell to us as the women would be angry if he did so.... This village is composed of refugees from Lake Leopold ii region; the people are Tumbas."
