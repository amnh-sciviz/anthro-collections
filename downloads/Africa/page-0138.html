Curatorial Notes: This is a carved wooden stool from the Makonde (Mawia) people in East Africa. The supports are in the shape of "two Makonde men in European clothes and two Makonde women in wrappers". The Makonde people of Tanzania and northern Mozambique have had a resilient history, resisting attempts to seize control of their land and people from nearby tribes, Middle Eastern groups, and European slavers. - Bortolot, Alexander Ives. Revolutions: a century of Makonde masquerade in Mozambique. New York: Miriam and Ira D. Wallach Art Gallery, Columbia University, 2007.
