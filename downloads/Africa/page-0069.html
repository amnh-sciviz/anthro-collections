Field Notes: "Tops - sea beans - green tips, Ntumba, Ikoko, 1.- [for group]" (491). 30 June 1906. Starting out his lengthy description of the objects collected on this date, Starr writes, "... we went to the [Ntumba] village where we secured considerable in games and pottery" (493). Starr's description of the tops game is included in a list of games collected that day: "ndsoko: spinning tops; a slight hollow is excavated; it is covered with pieces of plantain leaf; tops are spun in and the effort is to knock the opponent's top from the field; should both go off no gain; nor if both stay on; the one knocked off is lost" (497).

Field Notes: "Whistle to charm wealth. Ngombe, .10" (550). 22 August 1906. "We found wooden whistles of extremely simple construction--merely a neatly shaped long block with a round hole deeply excavated in it: the exact form varied but near the end all were pierced for suspension. The larger ones are usually hung singly to a neck-cord: the smaller ones are hung two, three or four together, to a neckcord or to an arm ring. All have the aim of making the wearer rich and are bwanga. If one, who knows a man has a fine knife or spear or money or other desired valued possession, blows upon this near that man's house, he will give him his heart's desire" (588).
