Curatorial Notes: This is a Kuba basket from Central Africa. The Kuba Kingdom, from what is now the southeastern part of the Congo, was strongest in the mid-nineteenth century. It was comprised of many culture groups, including the Bushoong, Shoowa, Kel, and Ngoombe. It was taken over by the Nsapo people in the late nineteenth century and declined afterwards. The Kuba are also referred to as the "Children of Woot", in reference to the deity Woot, of the Kuba religion.
