Field Notes: "Clapping sticks (pair). Bafoma. .61" (645). 1 October 1906. Starr purchases "Three pair of the hollowed clapping sticks, which are only from Bafoma so far as I have seen them" (681). Previously, on 26 September 1906, he got to see these sticks being used during a dance at Yakusu: "At one of the clubhouses a crowd was gathered and in the road before it was a group of Foma dancers. They each had a pair of hollowed sticks painted red, which they clapped against each other, producing a really excellent dance time. There were some fifteen or twenty men and women. The dance was lively, in a circle, not evidently suggestive; from time to time a man would dance into the circle and go through lively, more or less grotesque movements. To my surprise two men in the clubhouse ordered me to move on. On my refusing and talking the matter up rather vigorously a signal was given and the dancers vanished. At this I was very angry and ordered their return, which after delay took place. I stayed just long enough to see the thing renewed and then went on with my purchasing trip" (675).

The dance was lively, in a circle, not evidently suggestive; from time to time a man would dance into the circle and go through lively, more or less grotesque movements. To my surprise two men in the clubhouse ordered me to move on. On my refusing and talking the matter up rather vigorously a signal was given and the dancers vanished. At this I was very angry and ordered their return, which after delay took place. I stayed just long enough to see the thing renewed and then went on with my purchasing trip" (675).
