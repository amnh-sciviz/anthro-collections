POTTERY ANTHROPOMORPHIC JAR FOR BREWING BEER
SOUTH AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 40.0/ 9468
Culture: SHIPIBO
Locale: BAJO UCAYALI RIVER
Country: PERU
Date: Early/Mid 1900s
Material: CLAY, PIGMENT, GLAZE
Dimensions: L:29 W:28 H:31.5 (in CM)
Technique: POTTERY, DECORATED W/ INCISIONS & GEOMETRIC DESIGNS PAINTED ON CREAM BASE, GLAZED
Acquisition Year: 1957 [PURCHASE]
Donor: DE LOS RÍOS, RAÚL
Keywords: BEER JAR, EFFIGY JAR, POTTERY
Categories: CONTAINERS, EQUIPMENT
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This large pottery jar in human form for brewing beer is a good example of the masterly work of Shipibo potters. Women make the native beer from maize kernels or manioc tubers which they first boil, mash and masticate then let brew in a dark corner for 2 or 3 days. The alcohol content of this beer is low, usually under 2.5%. Collected by Raúl de los Ríos during his 23 years in Peruvian Amazonia in late 1920s through 1940s. The jar and 265 other objects were purchased by AMNH in 1957.
