Curatorial Notes: This Waiwai man's vanity basket with a cover was twill-plaited from split cane in the design called suhuí that refers to a weapon with magical qualities. Men weave these baskets and carry their face paints, feather decorations, etc. finery in them. The basket was collected by William H. Holden, MD on a medical research expedition to British Guiana (now Guyana) in 1938 and donated to AMNH in 1939.
