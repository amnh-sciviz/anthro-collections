Exhibition History
THE HORSE AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. May 2008 - January 2009
Exhibition Label: Argentina. Early 1900s. Ivory, copper alloy, silver, hide, rawhide. To stop cattle in their tracks, Argentine gauchos carried bolas, hunting weapons used by the native people of the South American plains. Three lengths of rope were bound together, with balls attached to the ends. Holding onto one ball, a gaucho whirled the others around his head and then flung the bolas. The ropes whipped around an animals legs so it fell to the ground.
