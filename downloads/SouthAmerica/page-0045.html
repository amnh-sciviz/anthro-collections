Curatorial Notes: This type of cactus thorns were used by Pilagá women in tattooing complex geometric patterns on their faces. Started in childhood, the tattoos were completed much later when the woman was ready to marry. The artist, usually an old woman, traced the outline of the design with charcoal and then punctured the skin with thorns like these, dipped in a mix of soot and saliva. These thorns are part of a large collection (over a 1000 pieces) from the Indians of the Argentine Chaco made by Alfred Métraux during an AMNH expedition in 1939.
