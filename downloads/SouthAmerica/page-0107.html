Curatorial Notes: Chest pendant of white metal attached to a choker worn by Mapuche (Araucanian) women. This type of metal jewelry, common among these Indians, was made by Mapuche silversmiths from coins or other silver alloy traded from the Spanish. This choker with pendant is part of a small collection of Mapuche jewelry donated to AMNH by the Estate of Luis O. Lewin in 1988.

Curatorial Notes: This bark cloth mask costume was made in 1970 and "danced" during Mourning Rites (oyno "weeping"). The Kubeo customarily burn these costumes at the end of the ceremony to allow the spirits to return to their original "home," but they allowed anthropologist Irving Goldman to take this and four others to his home in the U.S. The 1970 oyno ceremony which Goldman witnessed was presented first time in some 30 years. Goldman donated the mask costumes to AMNH in 1988.
