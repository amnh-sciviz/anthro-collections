Curatorial Notes: The Amahuaca made nose ornaments of tortoise shell to which small beads of eggshell were affixed with beeswax/resin mix. Men and women both wore these ornaments hanging below the nose with the cotton cord going through a hole in the nasal septum. This is one of hundreds of artifacts Raúl de los Ríos collected during his 23 years in Amazonia, from the late 1920s through 1940s. He donated the collection of more than 2000 items to AMNH in 1951 through his Munitalp Foundation.
