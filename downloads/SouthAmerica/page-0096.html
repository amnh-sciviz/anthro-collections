Curatorial Notes: This wind instrument, poári, sometimes called an idioglottal "clarinet," is made of a gourd covered with feathers. The mouthpiece is a narrow reed with a small slit flap as the "tongue." Its open end is placed into the gourd resonator. The slit flap is attached at one end, thus it pulses and makes air vibrations when air is blown over it. Vladimir Kozák collected this poári among the Bororo in 1957.

Curatorial Notes: A bullroarer is a flat piece of wood often carved in a form of a fish and decorated with geometric design. When a man hurls it from a long cord (here wound around the bullroarer) above his head, the bullroarer makes a very loud humming sound, the voice of its spirit, the Xingú River Indians say. Collected by Vladimír Kozák, Czech-Brazilian engineer, purchased by AMNH in 1971.
