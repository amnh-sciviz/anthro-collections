Curatorial Notes: Each of this pair of chisels has an agouti tooth affixed to a wooden handle and attached by a cord. Yanomamö men use these chisels to sharpen arrowpoints. They usually come in pairs. Part of 32 pieces collected by Bassett Maguire, New York Botanical Gardens, on a joint expedition with AMNH Dept. of Mammalogy in 1952-1953.
