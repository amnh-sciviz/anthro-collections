Curatorial Notes: Twill-plaited basketry has been common throughout South America and the Yekuana, masters of this intricate craft, still practice it. Woven from strips of split cane, some natural color, some blackened with charcoal, baskets and basketry trays have traditional patterns, in this case a frog or toad design. Men are the basket makers among the Yekuana. Part of a large collection of Amazonian material (339 items) bought at the Brazilian Indian Service depot by Robin G. Adair, a computer consultant, in 1977-1983. Mr. Adair donated the collection to AMNH in 1999.
