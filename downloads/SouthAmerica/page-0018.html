The Ona wore them primarily during the cold months of the southern winter. These shoes are part of a collection of 179 artifacts made by Colonel Charles W. Acquisition Year: 1909 [PURCHASE]
Donor: FURLONG, CHARLES W.
Keywords: SPUR

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: The Tehuelche (Aónikenk), on the High Pampas of Patagonia, adopted the horse in the early 1700s after the Spanish brought horses to the New World. These Indians developed an equestrian culture in many respects similar to the North American Plains cultures. This pair of spurs is made of wood and leather with sharpened metal points. Collected by Colonel Charles W. Furlong on an expedition to Tierra del Fuego in 1907-1908 funded by Harper's magazine. The spurs are on display in the Hall of South American Peoples.
