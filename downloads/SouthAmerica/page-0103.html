SOUTH AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION

Categories: CONTAINERS, WEAPONS AND AMMUNITION
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This bamboo quiver contains darts for the blowgun used to hunt monkeys and birds. The darts are tipped with curare, a powerful muscle relaxant derived from a jungle vine which makes the animal lose its grip in the tree. The calabash attached to the quiver contains silk cotton (Ceiba sp.) that the hunter wraps around the distal end of the dart to help steady its flight through the long blowgun barrel. Part of a large collection (346 artifacts) from the Waorani made during an expedition which was organized and led by Grant Behrman, an investment broker, in 1983. The quiver is on exhibit in the Hall of South American Peoples.

Exhibition History
THE POWER OF POISON AMNH, New York, NY. November 2013 - August 2014
Exhibition Label: Blowgun darts, quiver and fiber container. Hunters would coat these darts with the plant-based toxin known as curare. The powerful paralytic causes birds to "turn giddy and drop in a few seconds," according to one early naturalist. Fine plant fiber wound around the dart ends insures a snug fit in the blowgun tube and gives the hunter's breath a surface to act against. The round container, a gourd, holds extra fiber.
Bamboo, beeswax, plant fiber. Ecuador, South America.
