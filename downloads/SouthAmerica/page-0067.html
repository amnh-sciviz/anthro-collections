Acquisition Year: 1955 [PURCHASE]

Keywords: CASSAVA GRATER, MANIOC GRATER
Category: EQUIPMENT

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This manioc grater is made of wood with thousands of tiny sharp stone chips hammered into it and coated with resin and wax. The decorative designs, including the lizard, were painted by a man, the stones were set by women. After they've washed the manioc tubers, Waiwai and other Guianas area women grate them on graters like this one. The resulting pulp is then compressed into a manioc squeezer, a long tube which acts like a Chinese finger trap to remove the poisonous prussic acid. Collected in 1954 by Dr. C.R. Jones, a medical officer in British Guiana, and purchased with 179 other items by AMNH in 1955.
