Curatorial Notes: An old-style Karajá ceramic doll showing a female with exaggerated buttocks, stumps for arms and a hole for a navel. Her massive hairpiece is a long roll of black wax obtained from the underground nests of the stingless bees Melipona lineata Lep. There are no facial features, only two black circles to indicate the traditional scarification on Karajá Indians' cheeks. Most of these ceramic figurines were children's toys. Both boys and girls played at adult ceremonies using these dolls. Now the Karajá make realistic looking ceramic dolls and animal figures for sale. This figurine is part of a collection of 357 Amazonian Indian artifacts AMNH acquired in 1950 in an exchange from the Museu Paulista, São Paulo, Brazil.
