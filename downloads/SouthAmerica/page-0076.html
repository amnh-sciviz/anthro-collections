Curatorial Notes: These wooden spatulas or cake turners with geometric decoration are used by women to turn over the manioc pancakes (beijú cakes) on the large ceramic griddle they use to prepare this staple of most Amazonian Indians. They cultivate many varieties some of which can be eaten with little preparation while others must be processed to remove the lethal prussic acid before they are safe to eat. The tubers are washed, grated, the poisonous liquid squeezed out and then the flour can be made into cakes. This spatula, part of a collection of 234 pieces, was acquired by AMNH curator emeritus Robert L. Carneiro and Gertrude E. Dole during their fieldwork among the Kuikuru in 1953-1954.
