Curatorial Notes: Ceramic pipe with a metal stem for smoking tobacco which is widely used by Indians of Amazonia. It is ground up for use as snuff, smoked as cigarettes or cigars and in pipes. Shamans use it in their healing practice but tobacco is also smoked recreationally. This is one of the over 2000 artifacts Raúl de los Ríos collected during his 23 years in Amazonia, from the late 1920s through 1940s. He donated the pipe to AMNH through his foundation, Munitalp Foundation.
