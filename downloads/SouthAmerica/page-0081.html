Donor: SCHULTZ, HARALD
Keywords: DOLL FIGURE
Category: RECREATIONAL ARTIFACTS

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This bark cloth doll representing a monkey was carried by a masked dancer at a girl's puberty ceremony. After her first menstruation, a Tikuna girl goes through a ceremony lasting three or four days. Decorated with red and black paint and feathers, she will be the center of attention. Men wearing large, grotesque or humorous masks and carrying dolls like this one, beat drums, shake rattles and make mock threats demanding meat and manioc beer from the hosts. After the ceremony, the doll is given to children to play with. Collected by Harald Schultz, who witnessed this Tikuna ceremony, and purchased by AMNH in 1958.

Dimensions: IN BAG) L:100 W:68 H:42 (in CM)
Subject: OMA (FATHER OF THE WIND OR DEMON OF THE WIND)
Acquisition Year: 1958 [PURCHASE]
Donor: SCHULTZ, HARALD
Keywords: HEAD MASK
Category: COSTUME
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: Called oma, Father or Demon of the Wind, this mask was worn by a man at a girl's puberty ceremony. After her first menstruation, a Tikuna girl goes through a ceremony lasting three or four days which marks her transition into womanhood. Decorated with red and black paint and dressed in feather finery, she will be the center of round-the-clock feasting and dancing, the only time in her life. Men wearing large, grotesque masks like this one, beat drums, shake rattles and make mock threats demanding meat and manioc beer from the hosts. Harald Schultz witnessed this ceremony and collected the complete set of Tikuna ceremonial regalia, including this mask. Part of 218 Tikuna items AMNH purchased from Mr. Schultz in 1958.
