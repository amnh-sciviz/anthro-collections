DANCING BELT
SOUTH AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 40.0/ 2121
Culture: JVARO (SHUAR)
Locale: EAST ECUADOR
Country: ECUADOR
Date: Early 1920s
Material: BEAD (JOB'S TEAR), SHELL, COTTON
Dimensions: L:46 W:19 H:6.5 (in CM)
Acquisition Year: 1925 [GIFT]
Donor: LIDDLE
Keywords: DANCING BELT
Category: COSTUME
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: Jvaro (Shuar) women wear these belts over their cotton dresses for dancing. Both the belts and the dresses as well as men's kilts are woven from native cotton by men. In Jvaro society, men do the spinning, dyeing and weaving. The dancing belts are decorated with Job's tears beads and sections of snail shells which make a clear and loud sound when the women dance. C. Lester Liddle, a gold mine surveyor, collected this belt in the early 1920s and donated it to AMNH in 1925.
