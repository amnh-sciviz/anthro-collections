Curatorial Notes: This water jug, the work of a Pilagá woman potter, was carried on the back with the plant fiber cord acting as the tumpline. The cord passes through the handles and is kept in place by a groove around the pot at the level of the handles. The collector of this and more than 1000 other pieces from the Indians of the Argentinian Chaco, Alfred Métraux, was born in Switzerland and grew up in Argentina. He brought this collection to AMNH in 1939.
