Date: 1966-1967
Material: FEATHER (TOUCAN, CURASSOW), CORD (COTTON)
Dimensions: L:43 W:26 H:6 (in CM)
Technique: NETTED BASE
Acquisition Year: 2001 [PURCHASE]
Donor: LOUTHIAN, JAY
Keywords: HEADDRESS
Category: COSTUME

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: A headdress with toucan and curassow feathers attached to a netted base worn by Jívaro men across the forehead, as everyday finery. Part of a large collection which Jay and Edith Louthian made during the many years they lived in the Montaña region. This headdress was collected about 1967 by Hector Guimet for Jay Louthian, gallery owner in Iquitos, Peru, and purchased by AMNH in 2001.
