Exhibition History
OUR GLOBAL KITCHEN: FOOD, NATURE, CULTURE AMNH, NEW YORK, NEW YORK. November 2012 - August 2013
Exhibition Label: This pot belonged to the Moche people, who flourished more than 1,200 years ago in northern Peru. The pot was shaped to look like harvested produce: pacay pods (Inga feuilli), legumes cultivated for their sweet, cottony pulp. These images of food were placed in graves, perhaps as symbolic nourishment for the dead.

Exhibition History
WHALES: GIANTS OF THE DEEP AMNH, NEW YORK, NEW YORK. March 2013 - January 2014
Exhibition Label: More than 1,300 years ago in South America, before the rise of the Inca Empire, a people known as the Nasca lived in southern Peru. They decorated their pottery with colorful and elaborate figures, including dangerous-looking killer whales. On this ceramic trumpet, a killer whale with a humanlike hand carries a severed head - a symbol of military power.
MYTHIC CREATURES: DRAGONS, UNICORNS & MERMAIDS AMNH, NEW YORK, NY. May 2007
Exhibition Label: KILLER WHALE TRUMPET. Ceramic. Peru. 300 BC to AD 800. The picture on this ceramic trumpet shows the Nasca mythic killer whale.
