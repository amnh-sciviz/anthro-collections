DART QUIVER WITH DARTS, PIRANHA MANDIBLE, AND BAG
SOUTH AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION

Technique: CROCHETED BAG
Acquisition Year: 1973 [GIFT]

Keywords: BAG, DART, DART QUIVER, PIRANHA MANDIBLE
Categories: CONTAINERS, WEAPONS AND AMMUNITION

Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This Yagua dart quiver is made of palm leaf strips. The darts are for the blowgun used to hunt monkeys and birds. The darts are tipped with curare, a powerful muscle relaxant derived from a jungle vine which makes the animal lose its grip in the tree. Attached to the quiver is a bag crocheted of cotton thread and containing silk cotton (Ceiba sp.) that the hunter wraps around the distal end of the dart to help steady its flight through the long blowgun. This palm leaf quiver is made only by the Yagua and thus is a diagnostic feature of their culture. Acquired by Jay and Edith Louthian who ran a gallery in Iquitos, Peru from the late 1960s through early 1970s. Donated to AMNH in 1973. This dart quiver is on display in the Hall of South American Peoples.
