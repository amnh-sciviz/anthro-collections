Curatorial Notes: Two Kayap men dress in anteater costumes like this one for the children's naming ceremony which lasts for several weeks. They move around the village in a slow and stately dance. Friendly at first, they grow nastier as the time passes and their costumes turn shabbier. The festival ends with ceremonial naming of the children. Robin G. Adair, computer consultant, bought this mask costume among 338 other artifacts at the Brazilian Indian Service depot in 1977-1983. He donated it to AMNH in 1999.
