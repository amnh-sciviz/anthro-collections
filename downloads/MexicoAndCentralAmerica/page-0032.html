Curatorial Notes: Hollow, ceramic dog figure in the Colima style. This figure is also a pouring vessel; the tail functions as a spout. The dog represented is a hairless, voiceless breed, named the techichi or tepescuintli, developed in pre-Columbian Mexico and raised primarily for food. These vessels are usually found in the shaft-and-chamber tombs of West Mexico.
