JADE FIGURE
MEXICAN & CENTRAL AMERICAN ARCHAEOLOGICAL COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 30.1/ 2168
Country: MEXICO
Period: FORMATIVE, circa B.C. 1500 - A.D. 1 (OLMEC, EARLY-MIDDLE FORMATIVE B.C.1500-400)
Material: JADE
Dimensions: L: 9.5 W 5.0 D: 5.0 (in CM)
Subject: FIGURE, JADE
Acquisition Year: 1932 [PURCHASE]
Donor: KEARNEY, C. H.
Keywords: STONE FIGURINE
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: This jade figurine represents a seated were-jaguar, wearing a headdress or helmet. Were-jaguars are half-human, half-jaguar creatures that are often found in Olmec style art. Jaguars were considered to be very powerful animals and were often associated with the ruling elite, as well as with shamans who had to ability to transition between the human and spiritural world. This artifact is characterized by Olmec style features, but was collected in the state of Puebla.
