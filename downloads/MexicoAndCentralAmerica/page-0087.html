Curatorial Notes: Plumbate vessel in bird effigy shape. Molded bird head and tail attached to olla shaped vessel. Incised design on exterior of vessel. Orange clay pottery vessel with Plumbate slip. Tohil Plumbate pottery is often associated with the Toltec culture from Central Mexico, but was produced along that Pacific Coast near the Mexico-Guatemala border and is found widely throughout Mesoamerica during the Early Postclassic. It is unknown where this vessel was collected. Plumbate received its name due to the mistaken belief that these ceramics were covered in a lead glaze. In fact, a high-alumina, high-iron slip is applied to the exterior surface of these vessels. When fired at high temperatures in a reduced environment, the slip vitrifies and produces a glassy sheen similar in appearance to lead glazes.
