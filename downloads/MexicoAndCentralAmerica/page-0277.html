BUST OF GENERAL DOUGLAS MACARTHUR
MEXICAN & CENTRAL AMERICAN ETHNOGRAPHIC COLLECTION
 
Catalog No: 65 / 5604
Culture: CUNA
Locale: USTUPPU ISLAND
Country: PANAMA
Date: Mid 1900s
Material: WOOD (BALSA), PLASTER, PIGMENT
Dimensions: L:46 W:24 H:20 (in CM)
Acquisition Year: 1958 [PURCHASE]
Donor: AGNEW, LOUISE
Keywords: BUST
Share Object
Add Object to Portfolio
Curatorial Notes: The inspiration for this balsa wood carving of General MacArthur came from the American troops stationed in the area during WWII who told the Kuna that General MacArthur was "a great and strong man" who could protect them. The figure of the General was seen as having a protective spirit. It was originally a tall figure close to 2.5 meters high. By the time Louise Agnew found it, much of it had been destroyed by termites and the Kuna had sawed off the decayed part. Ms. Agnew acquired this bust of General MacArthur and 75 other Kuna items on her extensive travels on the San Blas Islands, the Museum purchased it from her in 1958.
