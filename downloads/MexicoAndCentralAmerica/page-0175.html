Curatorial Notes: Conical-shaped pottery vessel; orange clay with red-orange slip and black slip; streaky burnishing on exterior. Described in the AMNH catalog as a vase for drinking pulque, a fermented beverage made from the agave plant. Locale in catalog given as "Nonoalco, Camp Cerro, Depot of Mexican Light and Power Company between Calzada de Nonoalco and last block of calle de Marte, San Barolito, grave selection."
Exhibition History
OUR GLOBAL KITCHEN: FOOD, NATURE, CULTURE AMNH, NEW YORK, NEW YORK. November 2012 - August 2013
Exhibition Label: The sweet sap of the maguey, an agave plant, was used as syrup and fermented into an alcoholic drink called pulque, which one Spaniard described admiringly as "a sweet and clean wine." This cup is a shape traditionally used for drinking pulque during Aztec times.
